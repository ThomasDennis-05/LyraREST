      SOCIAL ENGINEERING AWARENESS

      Social engineering attacks target human psychology rather than technical vulnerabilities. They're often the first step in a broader cyber attack.

      Always be cautious when:

      • Someone creates a sense of urgency or fear
      • You're asked for sensitive information like passwords or personal details
      • You receive unexpected communications with suspicious links or attachments
      • Someone uses excessive authority or pressure tactics
      • Email addresses don't match official company domains
      • You're asked to bypass normal security procedures

      Remember: Legitimate company representatives will never ask for your password or pressure you into immediate action that bypasses security protocols.
